How to use:

  • The "Basic Moves" menu on the right hand side contains a list of moves that are possible to carry out on a Rubik's Cube. You can expand this menu by clicking "Show More Moves". Click one to see the move in action!
  • You can drag about the centre cube to have a look at all its sides
  • Click "Scramble" at the bottom of the page to apply a random scramble to the cube.
  • Then, click "Solve" and a calculated solution will appear on the screen
  • Pressing "Play" will show you the solution to the scramble and you can pause it at any time. You can also use the arrow buttons to step forwards or backwards a step in case you get lost.
  • There are two bars at the bottom. The top bar is a progress bar and shows you how far through the solution you are. The second controls the speed of the animation so you can slow it down or speed it up as you like.
  • The left hand side shows some helpful features.
    • "How to use this program" will bring up this box for if you ever need a reminder of the features
    • "Notation Definitions" will display a popup with the definitions of all the notations you see
    • Clicking "Type Scramble" will bring up an input box where you can type in a scramble. Click "Submit" to apply it to the cube
    • "Show back sides" will show you a projection of the back sides of the cube so you always know what is going on on the other side. Click the button to see and click the button again to toggle it off.
    • If the notation format of the solution is hard to understand, you can click "Display Long Instructions" to replace the notation format solution with easier to understand instructions

Notation Definitions:

  • Cubing notation involves the letters R, U, L, D, B and F (and this list can be extended to include M, E, S, X, Y, Z) where each letter refers to a different face of the cube.
    • R : right
    • L : left
    • U : up (top)
    • D : down (bottom)
    • F : front
    • B : back
  • Each of these letters can come in three forms (R, R', R2).
    • A letter on its own refers to a 90 degree clockwise rotation of that layer
    • An apostrophe refers to the inverse of the move, and hence a 90 degree anticlockwise rotation
    • The number 2 refers to a double rotation, and hence a 180 degree rotation
